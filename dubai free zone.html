Companies in Free Zones generally enjoy a 0% corporate tax on qualifying income, as long as they meet specific criteria set by the UAE Corporate Tax Law, effective from June 2023. Tax Benefits in Dubai Free Zones

To benefit from corporate tax exemptions in Dubai’s Free Zones, companies must meet specific criteria established by the UAE’s Corporate Tax Law, effective from June 2023. Here are the primary requirements:

  • Adequate Economic Substance: Companies must demonstrate a substantial economic presence within the Free Zone. This typically includes having a minimum number of employees, conducting core income-generating activities within the Free Zone, and possessing sufficient resources to support these activities.
  • Qualifying Income: Only income deemed “qualifying” by the Free Zone Authority is eligible for tax exemption. Qualifying income generally includes revenue from activities conducted within the Free Zone or from international business. Non-qualifying income, including any income from local (UAE mainland) transactions, may be taxed at the standard rate of 9% if it exceeds either 5% of total company revenue or AED 5 million, whichever is lower.
  • Registration and Compliance: Companies must be registered with the relevant Free Zone Authority and comply with all its regulations, including maintaining accurate records, submitting tax filings, and following local business regulations.
  • Adherence to UAE Corporate Tax Law: All activities must be conducted according to UAE tax laws and regulations, including requirements for documentation and periodic reporting to remain eligible for the tax benefits.

In addition to tax exemptions, Dubai Free Zones provide customs duty waivers, 100% foreign ownership, and no personal income tax.
